What Siri is — and where it stops

At WWDC 2026, Apple unveiled a next-generation Siri positioned as a more capable, agentic assistant across Apple platforms. Reviews note faster responses and better contextual help, especially inside iOS, CarPlay, and macOS apps [forbes.com].

Siri shines when your work lives inside Apple’s ecosystem. It’s optimized for device-level assistance, privacy constraints, and Apple apps—not for repeatedly driving arbitrary desktop workflows.

Siri

  • Tightly integrated with iOS, macOS, and Apple services
  • Great for reminders, navigation, messaging, and system actions
  • Limited ability to reuse prior computer interactions

Super

  • Personal AI agents that operate real computers
  • Designed for browser, SaaS, and desktop workflows
  • Reuses a computer-use cache for repeated tasks

Why Super feels sharper for real work

Operate the computer

Super’s agents interact with real UIs—clicking, typing, navigating—similar to emerging “computer use” agents now seen in systems like Google Gemini [blog.google].

Remember past runs

Instead of starting from scratch every time, Super reuses prior steps via a computer-use cache—making repeated workflows faster and more cost-efficient.

Built for repetition

If you run the same reports, onboarding flows, or data pulls weekly, Super is intentionally better suited than voice-first assistants.

How Super fits into the broader AI agent landscape

Tools like ChatGPT, Gemini, and Grok are expanding what conversational AI can do. Products such as Folk and Orchids explore CRM-style or workflow-focused agents. Siri, by contrast, remains primarily a system assistant.

Super’s niche is clear: a personal AI agent that repeatedly uses computers on your behalf—and gets better the more you run the same work.
